Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, disclose and protect your personal and credit information.

Last updated: June 2026

Coyne Financial Pty Ltd (“Coyne Finance”, “we”, “us”, “our”) is committed to protecting the privacy of the personal information we hold. This policy explains how we handle personal information and credit-related information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) and the Privacy (Credit Reporting) Code, where applicable.

Coyne Finance operates as a Credit Representative (Credit Representative Number [XXXXXX]) of AFAS Group Pty Ltd, Australian Credit Licence 414426.

The information we collect

Depending on your enquiry, we may collect:

  • identity and contact details (such as name, phone, email and address);
  • financial and employment information relevant to assessing a finance application;
  • details of the asset you wish to finance and the proposed transaction;
  • credit information and credit eligibility information, where you authorise us to obtain it.

How we collect it

We generally collect information directly from you — for example, through our enquiry form, by phone or by email. With your consent, we may also collect information from credit reporting bodies, lenders, your nominated referees and other third parties relevant to your application.

Why we collect, hold, use and disclose it

We use your information to assess your finance needs, prepare and submit applications to lenders, arrange and manage credit, and to comply with our legal and regulatory obligations. We may disclose your information to lenders and aggregators (including AFAS Group Pty Ltd), credit reporting bodies, our service providers, and others as required or authorised by law.

Disclosure to credit reporting bodies

Where relevant, lenders may disclose your information to, and obtain information from, credit reporting bodies. Information about how those bodies handle your information, and your rights (including to request that they not use your information for direct marketing or pre-screening), is set out in their privacy policies.

Direct marketing

We may, where permitted, contact you with information about products and services that may be relevant to you. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by contacting us using the details below.

Keeping your information secure

We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, modification or disclosure.

Accessing and correcting your information

You may request access to the personal information we hold about you, and ask us to correct it if it is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, irrelevant or misleading. Contact us using the details below.

Complaints

If you have a privacy concern or complaint, please contact us first so we can try to resolve it. You can also refer to our Complaints & Dispute Resolution page. If your complaint is not resolved to your satisfaction, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.
